# SEO Checklist

Run this checklist after drafting a blog post. Every item is a pass/fail gate.

---

## Title

- [ ] 50-60 characters (including spaces)
- [ ] Primary keyword appears in the first half of the title
- [ ] Descriptive and specific — not clickbait or vague
- [ ] Uses natural language (not keyword-stuffed)
- [ ] No ALL CAPS, no excessive punctuation

## Slug (URL)

- [ ] Derived from title, lowercase, words separated by hyphens
- [ ] Under 60 characters
- [ ] Contains primary keyword
- [ ] No stop words unless needed for clarity (a, the, in, on)

## Meta Description (front matter `description` field)

- [ ] 150-160 characters (Google truncates longer ones)
- [ ] Contains primary keyword (gets bolded in search results)
- [ ] Promises clear value — what the reader will learn or gain
- [ ] Written as a complete, compelling sentence
- [ ] Not duplicated from the opening paragraph

## Headings

- [ ] Primary keyword appears in at least one H2
- [ ] Headings are descriptive and front-loaded with key terms
- [ ] Heading hierarchy is valid (H2 -> H3, no skipped levels)

## Content

- [ ] Primary keyword appears naturally in the first 100 words
- [ ] Keywords appear naturally throughout — not forced or repeated excessively
- [ ] Content is comprehensive enough to be the best resource on the topic

## Images

- [ ] All images have alt text (80-125 characters)
- [ ] Alt text describes the image's meaning in context, not just the subject
- [ ] Never starts with "Image of..." or "Picture of..."
- [ ] Alt text includes relevant keywords where natural
- [ ] Image filenames are descriptive kebab-case (not IMG_1234.png)

## Internal Links

- [ ] At least 2 internal links to related existing posts
- [ ] Anchor text is descriptive (not "click here" or "read more")
- [ ] Links are contextually relevant, not forced

## Tags and Categories

- [ ] 5-8 tags, lowercase, hyphenated
- [ ] Tags are searchable terms readers would actually look for
- [ ] 2-3 categories from the blog's established set
- [ ] No duplicate meaning between tags (e.g., don't use both "ai" and "artificial-intelligence")
